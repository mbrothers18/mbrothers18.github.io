WxFL Guidelines

 

     1.         League Overview

                  1.1.         The league’s season is split up into three meteorological seasons: Winter (Dec-Feb), Spring (Mar-May), and Summer (Jun-Aug). Fall (Sep-Nov) will be used for the playoffs and as the league’s offseason.

                  1.2.         Teams will consist of six cities (three starters and three bench). Acquisition of cities via draft, free agency, and trades are explained in Section 2.

                  1.3.         Each season will be scored based off of various meteorological phenomena occurring at cities defined in Section 3. Teams will be ranked against opposing teams in each of these categories for each month as well as their season total for select categories. Points will be awarded based on how close the team is to the leader in each category.

                                1.3.1.         Monthly Points

                                             1.3.1.1.         Each category winner recieves 1 point with the remainder of the field revieving their fraction of the winner’s value (i.e. if 1st place has 30 in of snow, 2nd place has 27 in, and 3rd place has 18 in, then the points awarded are 1, 0.9, and 0.6 respectively).

                                1.3.2.         Season Points

                                             1.3.2.1.         Each category winner receives 3 point with the remainder of the field receiving their fraction of the winner’s value multiplied by 3 (i.e. if 1st place has 30 in of snow, 2nd place has 27 in, and 3rd place has 18 in, then the points awarded are 3, 2.7, and 1.8 respectively).

                                1.3.3.         Additional Comments

                                             1.3.3.1.         No points are awarded for not recording in a category for a specific scoring period (i.e. tied for 1st with 0 in of snowfall does not award any points)

                  1.4.         The team with the most points (monthly and season) at the end of the season (Winter, Spring, and Summer) will be deemed the winner. The top three teams each season will earn the right to participate in WxFL’s postseason, The Gauntlet Playoffs, explained in Section 4.

     2.         Cities

                  2.1.         Cities Available

                                2.1.1.         A predefined list of CONUS cities have been made available for teams to select from. Cities are selected from those that the NWS makes daily climate reports for and span CONUS without too much clustering in certain areas.

                  2.2.         Roster

                                2.2.1.         Teams consist of three starting cities and three bench cities.

                                2.2.2.         Cities can be acquired by teams through drafts, free agency, and trades.

                                2.2.3.         Weekly roster changes may be made before 00z Monday for the upcoming week or by 00Z on the first day of the month.

                  2.3.         Draft

                                2.3.1.         An Auction style draft will occur in late November before the WxFL winter season begins. Each team is allocated $50 of spending money to bid on six teams to fill out their roster (~$8 per team). 

                                2.3.2.         Live or Blind Auction?

                  2.4.         Free Agency

                                2.4.1.         Free agent request may be made at any time. Once a request is made, the team must wait 24 hours or up until 2 hours before weekly roster deadline (whichever comes first) before the signing can be confirmed. The transaction can be cancelled anytime before the waiting period is over. If another team makes a request for the same city within this time period, then the waiver list will determine which team acquires the city.

                                2.4.2.         Free agency pickups will not join the team’s active roster until the next time rosters can be altered and will automatically replace the spot of the city it is being exchanged for (starter or bench) unless otherwise informed by the team.

                                2.4.3.         Once a team picks up a city, they will be moved to the bottom of the waiver list. The waiver list will be reset at the beginning of each month as the reverse order of the previous month’s standings.

                  2.5.         Trades

                                2.5.1.         Teams are allowed to trade any number of cities to another team if an agreement can be reached. If there’s not a 1:1 ratio of trade pieces being exchanged, then you must submit accompanying roster moves (add/drop) so that your roster consists of exactly six cities.

                                2.5.2.         After both teams have reported the proposed trade, teams must wait 24 hours for the trade to become official. In those 24 hours, the rest of the league has the ability to veto the trade with a majority vote.

                                2.5.3.         Traded cities, like free agents, will not be available in the new team’s active lineup until the upcoming weekly roster change.

     3.         Season Scoring

                  3.1.         Winter

                                3.1.1.         Accumulated Snowfall (in) -- Monthly and Seasonal Scoring

                                3.1.2.         Maximum 24-hr Snowfall (in) -- Monthly and Seasonal (average of three months’ maximum 24-hr Snowfall) Scoring

                                3.1.3.         Cold Temperature Days (count of days with max temperature at or below 32F plus count of days with min temperature at or below 0F) -- Monthly Scoring

                                3.1.4.         Severe Weather Days (count of days with tornado, hail and/or wind reports within 100 miles) -- Monthly Scoring

                  3.2.         Spring

                                3.2.1.         Accumulated Snowfall (in) -- Monthly Scoring

                                3.2.2.         Accumulated QPF (in) -- Monthly Scoring

                                3.2.3.         Tornado Days (count of days with tornado reports within 50 miles) -- Monthly and Seasonal Scoring

                                3.2.4.         Severe Weather Days (count of days with hail and/or wind reports within 50 miles) -- Monthly and Seasonal Scoring

                  3.3.         Summer

                                3.3.1.         Accumulated QPF (in) -- Monthly and Seasonal Scoring

                                3.3.2.         Maximum 24-hr QPF (in) -- Monthly Scoring

                                3.3.3.         Hot Temperature Days (count of days with max temperature at or above 95F plus count of days with max temperature at or above 100F) -- Monthly Scoring

                                3.3.4.         Severe Weather Days (count of days with tornado, hail and/or wind reports within 75 miles) -- Monthly and Seasonal Scoring

                  3.4.         Additional Comments

                                3.4.1.         For severe weather verification, multiple counts can be awarded to a team on the same day if multiple cities verify (i.e. If two different cities verify on the same day, then two counts are awarded to the team).

                                             3.4.1.1.         Same thing applies to hot and cold temperature days

                                3.4.2.         For severe weather verification, multiple counts can be awarded to a team on the same day if one city verifies with different types of reports (i.e. if Jackson, MS verifies with both wind and hail reports on the same day, then two counts are awarded to the team).

     4.         The “Gauntlet” Playoffs

                  4.1.         The Gauntlet will serve as the postseason championship for the WxFL where teams will be awarded the ability to acquire a certain number of cities based on their performance in the previous three seasons and try to rack up the most “events” out of all teams participating in the playoffs. The Gauntlet Playoffs will begin a few days after the conclusion of the Summer season (~ Sept. 5th) and will run no later than mid October (~ Oct. 15th). The team that observes the most events among it’s cities will become the winner.

                  4.2.         Qualification

                                4.2.1.         At the end of each season (Winter, Spring, and Summer), teams will be awarded the ability to draft cities for The Gauntlet based on their placement. 1st place earns 3 cities, 2nd place earns 2 cities, and 3rd place earns 1 city. 

                  4.3.         Auction Draft

                                4.3.1.         Teams will be awarded a number of auction picks equivalent to the number earned over the course of all three seasons. Each team will receive $10 of auction money per draft pick earned throughout the three seasons.

                                4.3.2.         Teams will submit bids on cities in a blind auction format (without knowledge on other team’s bidding prices) to fill out their roster while remaining under their auction money allotment. Subsequent rounds of bidding will occur until all teams receive the number of cities they earned or run out of auction money. If two teams bid the same amount on a city, they will be notified to submit a new price in the subsequent rounds, but no other teams allowed to join the bidding.

                                4.3.3.         All cities from any season will be eligible to be bid on for the Gauntlet Playoffs. There is no free agency over the course of the postseason. Who you aquire is who you got until a winner is determined.

                  4.4.         Events (11)

                                4.4.1.         First Frost (Temp <= 32F)

                                4.4.2.         First 90F Day (Temp >= 90F)

                                4.4.3.         First Snowfall > 3 in

                                4.4.4.         First Rainfall > 0.5 in

                                4.4.5.         Nearest Location to First Hurricane Landfall

                                4.4.6.         First City Accumulated HDD > 75

                                4.4.7.         First City Accumulated CDD > 75

                                4.4.8.         First Wind Gust > 40 mph

                                4.4.9.         First Severe Weather Report within 100 miles (tor>hail>wind)

                             4.4.10.         First Thunder Report

                             4.4.11.         First Fog

                  4.5.         Tiebreakers

                                4.5.1.         If two cities both break the same category on the same day, the point goes to the city with the highest value (lowest for first frost). If still tied or if highest value cannot be determined from category, only those cities tied remain in the running for that category and the city that observes the event next earns the point.

                                4.5.2.         If an event does not occur by the end of the playoff period, the city with the closest value on the earliest date to the event earns the point.

                                             4.5.2.1.         In the case that a hurricane landfall does not occur in the time period, the event will be downgraded to Tropical Storm landfall. If a Tropical Storm landfall does not occur, then the event will become the city closest to any hurricane/tropical storm track over the ocean.

                                4.5.3.         If a tie in playoff events earned exist among teams (i.e. 3-3-3-2, 5-5-1, etc), then the teams tied for first will go head to head with the events earned by teams not tied for first until a winner is determined.
